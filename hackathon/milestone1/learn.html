Kadri Temple

The Kadri Hill is renowned for the 11th-century Kadri Manjunatha Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. This historical temple is considered the oldest Shiva Temple in Mangaluru, reflecting a rich heritage of religious and architectural significance.

The temple's architecture is a fascinating blend of Hindu and Buddhist styles, showcasing a unique fusion of cultural elements. The main deity of the temple is Manjunatha, a revered form of Lord Shiva, depicted in a majestic idol that attracts countless devotees.

The temple is also notable for its beautiful tanks filled with natural spring water, adding to the spiritual ambiance. The laterite caves, commonly referred to as the Pandava Caves, are an interesting feature of the temple complex. According to local legends, these caves were used by the Pandavas during their exile.

The Kadri Temple is a hub for vibrant cultural activities and celebrations. The annual Kadri Kambala, a traditional buffalo race, is a major event that draws visitors from far and wide. This festival not only highlights the local customs but also enhances the cultural richness of the region.
