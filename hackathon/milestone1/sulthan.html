Sulthan Battery

Sulthan Battery, also known as Sultan Battery, is a historic watchtower built by Tippu Sultan in the 18th century. Located in Mangaluru, it overlooks the picturesque Arabian Sea and offers a glimpse into the architectural prowess of the bygone era.

The watchtower was constructed to keep a lookout for enemy ships and to protect the coast. Its strategic location on the banks of the Gurupura River provided a vantage point for monitoring the surroundings. The structure is made of black stones and has a unique design with cannon openings and thick walls.

Today, Sulthan Battery is a popular tourist attraction, known for its historical significance and scenic beauty. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the river and the coastline from the watchtower. The area around Sulthan Battery is also ideal for leisurely strolls and offers insights into the rich history of Mangaluru.
